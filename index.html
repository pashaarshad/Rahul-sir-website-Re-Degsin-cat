Frequently Asked Questions

CAT Exam FAQ

Get answers to the most common questions about CAT preparation and exam strategy from our experts.

How can I crack CAT exam easily?

There's no "easy" way to crack CAT, but a systematic approach helps: Start with understanding the exam pattern, build strong fundamentals in all three sections (VARC, DILR, QA), practice regularly with mock tests, analyze your performance, work on time management, and maintain consistency. Focus on accuracy over speed initially, then gradually build pace.

What is the best way to improve CAT reading comprehension?

To improve RC: Read diverse content daily (newspapers, magazines, journals), practice active reading by summarizing paragraphs, work on increasing reading speed while maintaining comprehension, learn to identify main ideas and tone, practice different question types, and time yourself while solving RC passages. Focus on understanding the passage structure and author's perspective.

How many hours should I study daily for CAT?

For working professionals: 3-4 hours daily. For students: 6-8 hours daily. Quality matters more than quantity. Focus on productive study time with proper breaks. Include theory, practice, mock tests, and analysis in your daily routine. Maintain consistency rather than cramming. Adjust based on your strengths, weaknesses, and available time.

When should I start preparing for CAT?

Ideally, start 10-12 months before the exam. This gives you enough time to build strong fundamentals, practice extensively, and take multiple mock tests. If you're starting late, 6-8 months can work with intensive preparation. Early start allows for better concept building and stress-free preparation. Remember, it's never too early or too late to start!

How can I improve my accuracy in CAT exam?

Focus on solving questions correctly first, even if you solve fewer initially. Don't rush through questions. Read carefully, double-check your answers, and skip tricky ones if needed. Regular practice and thorough mock test analysis will naturally boost your accuracy over time.

How many times can I attempt the CAT exam?

There's no limit on the number of CAT attempts. You can take the exam multiple times, but most top B-schools consider only your latest or best score. However, it's recommended to prepare thoroughly for each attempt rather than relying on multiple chances. Each attempt should be treated as your final one for best results.

Does CAT have negative marking?

Yes, CAT has negative marking. For every wrong answer, 1/3rd (0.33) marks are deducted. This is why accuracy is crucial. It's better to attempt questions you're confident about rather than guessing randomly. Develop a strategy to identify questions you can solve accurately and skip the ones you're unsure about.
